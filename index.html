Hey, I’m Albert Sun!

I’m a sophomore at Duke University studying Data Science: Sociology and Racism, a self-designed Bachelor’s degree program. I’m interested in leveraging academic research and personal narratives to understand structural racism in the legal system.

What I’m doing now

I’ve been investigating the intersection between science, policy, and law as a Huang Fellow in Duke’s Science and Society Initiative. I’m working to create the Huang Fellows Racial Equity Committee and Initiative to invite black speakers/faculty and lead activist action within the Huang Fellows against America’s structural racism and recent murders of Ahmaud Arbery, George Floyd, Breonna Taylor.

In addition, I’m expanding my data science toolbox through Duke University’s Data Plus Summer Fellowship. I’m working with Duke Professor Astrid Giugni and a wonderful team to investigate the early histories of consumerism using Python scraping tools (concurrent, requests, bs4) and R packages (ggplot2, dplyr, tidytext, GloVe) to conduct Natural Language Processing, network analysis, and word embeddings.
