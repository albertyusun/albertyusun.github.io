Hey, I’m Albert Sun!

I’m a sophomore at Duke University studying Data Science: Sociology and Racism, a self-designed Bachelor’s degree program. I’m interested in leveraging data advocacy and personal narratives to combat structural racism in the legal system.

What I’m doing now

I’m investigating the intersection between science, policy, and law as a Huang Fellow in Duke’s Science and Society Initiative. I’m working to create the Huang Fellows Racial Equity Committee, and we're working with Professor Nita Farahany to make structural reforms in Duke S&S in response to recent acts of police violence. Huang Fellows.

In addition, I’m expanding my machine learning toolbox through Duke University’s Data Plus Summer Fellowship. I’m working with Duke Professor Astrid Giugni to investigate the early histories of consumerism. We're using Python and R (stm, GloVe) to conduct natural language processing, structural topic modeling, and word embeddings on 26,000 old English texts. GitHub. Project Information.

Finally, I've begun my op-ed journey for the Duke Chronicle. Check out my first piece which was featured as Top Story, "I wouldn’t exist without Black activism". Article.
