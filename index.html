What I’m doing now

I’m expanding my machine learning toolbox as a Duke University’s Data Plus Fellow. I’m working with a wonderful team led by Professor Astrid Giugni to investigate the early histories of consumerism. We're using TensorFlow, word2vec, and multiple other packages in Python and R to conduct structural topic modeling and word embeddings on 26,000 old English texts. GitHub. Project Info.

In addition, I’m investigating the intersection between science and law as a Huang Fellow in Duke’s Science and Society Initiative. A few of us have created the Huang Fellows Racial Equity Committee, and we're working to make structural reforms in Duke S&S in response to recent acts of racist police violence. Bio.

Finally, I've begun my op-ed journey for the Duke Chronicle. Check out my first piece which was featured as Top Story, "I wouldn’t exist without Black activism". Article.
