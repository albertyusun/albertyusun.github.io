Selected Projects

Right now, I'm doing a Research Independent Study advised by Professor Machanavajjhala to do research on differential privacy and fairness. Differential privacy is a privacy standard; I'm researching methods to make accessing differentially private datasets fair and effective. Learn what Differential Privacy is here: Differential Privacy

As a Data Engineering Intern at the Vera Institute of Justice, I had the opportunity to work on further developing the Incarceration Trends Project, the largest and most aggregate jail dataset in the country. Using Python, Google Cloud, and a suite of other database tools like SQLite, I developed data pipelines to scrape jail data from state and county websites to be displayed on the website. Website

Last summer, I founded and directed an internship program called Tech for Equity. I matched 24 students with paid internships at community-based organizations, managed a team of 4 project managers and scoped 10 technical internship projects (SWE, DS, PM) for interns. Our team raised $61k total funding for first summer program costs and fellowship stipends. News Coverage. Program Website.

In the spring, I competed in Duke's largest data competition, Duke DataFest (sponsored by the American Statistical Association). Our challenge was to create predictions on drug addiction in patients by using a convoluted medical dataset based on survey responses. My team won the Judges' Choice award for our work on emphasizing the importance on interpretable and explainable modeling in clinical settings. News Coverage

I worked on a Bass Connections Research Project led by Duke Law Professor Brandon Garrett to research the benefits for a publically-accessible online database for jails in North Carolina in the Wilson Center for Science and Justice. Project.

As a Data Plus Research Fellow and Huang Fellow, I expanded my machine learning and NLP toolbox. I worked with a team of Duke professors and graduate students to investigate the early histories of consumerism. We used word2vec and multiple other language models in Python and R to conduct structural topic modeling and word embeddings on 26,000 old English texts. GitHub. Project Info.
